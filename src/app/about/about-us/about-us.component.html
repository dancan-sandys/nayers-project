Ethics & Policies

Nayers Foundation is commitment to inclusion, diversity and safeguarding interest of needy children.

Our organisational culture

Nayers Foundation is committed to building a better world for needy children to grow in where each child is provided for treated with dignity and respect, free of harassment and accessing the opportunities needed for the Children to grow into thriving adults. Nayers Foundation has a special obligation to ensure the safety and dignity of all children it comes into contact with through its work. This policy reinforces our commitment to the best interests of children. This policy states our expectations regarding team member conduct with children and what conduct is prohibited. It also states the process and procedures we will maintain to ensure that team members uphold its standards and how cases of misconduct are identified, reported, and addressed in an appropriate and timely manner.

Financials

We carry out charitable activities and complement government efforts towards service delivery to the children and citizens at large. We believe that it is our duty to use the financial resources entrusted to us effectively. We monitor and evaluate the efficiency and effectiveness of our programmes with a dedicated staff of specialists and conduct regular internal and external financial audits.

Our Objectives

The main objective states in broad terms the purpose for which the organisation is formed.The main objective has been stated thus:

  1. To see all children and young persons leading a happy and fullfilling life.
  2. To promote and secure the rights of children and young persons in order for then to realise their dreams.
  3. To establish education fund mechanism to facilitate the intellectual capacity,equiping the society with the relevant knowledge.
  4. To protect and promote the best interests and welfare of children and young persons and enable them to overcome social problems through economic development of individuals,families and communities
  5. To advocate for and influence the public understanding of children basic and human rights and for the government budgetary allocation for children especiallyfor those in need of alternative family care eg Orphans.
